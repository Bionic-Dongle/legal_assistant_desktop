STATEMENT OF CLAIM
1. Parties

The Plaintiff, Arthur Hargreaves, is the lawful owner and occupier of the residential property situated at 14 Gumleaf Parade, Bottlebrush Shire.

The Defendant, Dennis Mulligan, is the lawful owner and occupier of the adjoining residential property situated at 16 Gumleaf Parade, Bottlebrush Shire.

2. The Tree

At all material times, a mature jacaranda tree (“the Tree”) was located entirely within the boundary of the Plaintiff’s property.

The Tree was planted more than thirty years ago and had substantial environmental, aesthetic, and sentimental value to the Plaintiff.

The Tree was healthy, structurally sound, and posed no immediate danger to persons or property prior to the events giving rise to this claim.

3. Encroachment of Roots

Over time, portions of the Tree’s root system naturally extended beneath the boundary line and into the soil beneath the Defendant’s property.

The Plaintiff was not notified of any alleged damage caused by the roots prior to the Defendant’s actions.

No expert assessment, arborist report, or formal notice was provided to the Plaintiff regarding the roots.

4. Defendant’s Conduct

On or about 12 March 2026, the Defendant intentionally introduced a toxic substance into the soil on his property in the immediate vicinity of the Tree’s root system.

The substance was introduced with the purpose of killing or severely damaging the roots extending from the Tree.

The Defendant undertook these actions without the Plaintiff’s knowledge, consent, or any lawful authority.

The Defendant did not confine his actions to pruning or lawful removal of roots and instead applied a chemical agent capable of systemic absorption.

5. Damage Caused

As a direct result of the Defendant’s actions, the Tree began to exhibit signs of rapid decline, including leaf loss, branch dieback, and structural instability.

Within weeks, the Tree was determined to be irreversibly damaged and subsequently died.

The death of the Tree required its full removal to prevent risk to persons and property.

The Plaintiff incurred costs associated with assessment, removal, and disposal of the Tree.

6. Loss and Harm

The Plaintiff has suffered financial loss, including but not limited to removal costs and loss of property value.

The Plaintiff has suffered loss of amenity and enjoyment of his property.

The Plaintiff has suffered emotional distress arising from the destruction of a tree of significant personal importance.

7. Cause of Action

The Defendant’s conduct constitutes:
a. Trespass to property, by intentionally interfering with vegetation beyond his lawful entitlement;
b. Negligence, by failing to take reasonable steps to avoid foreseeable harm;
c. Nuisance, by unlawfully interfering with the Plaintiff’s use and enjoyment of his land.

8. Relief Sought

The Plaintiff seeks the following relief:
a. Damages for the loss and removal of the Tree;
b. Compensation for loss of amenity and enjoyment;
c. An order restraining the Defendant from engaging in similar conduct in the future;
d. Costs of these proceedings;
e. Such further relief as the Court deems just.
