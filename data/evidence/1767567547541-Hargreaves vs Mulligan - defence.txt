DEFENCE
1. Preliminary

Except where expressly admitted, the Defendant denies each and every allegation contained in the Statement of Claim.

2. Parties

The Defendant admits paragraphs 1 and 2 of the Statement of Claim.

3. The Tree

The Defendant admits the existence of a tree located on the Plaintiff’s property.

The Defendant denies that the Tree was healthy, structurally sound, or posed no risk to property prior to the events alleged.

The Defendant says the Tree had caused ongoing and observable damage to paving, fencing, and subsurface drainage on the Defendant’s land.

4. Root Encroachment

The Defendant admits that the Tree’s roots extended substantially into the Defendant’s property.

The Defendant says the encroaching roots caused repeated cracking of paving and obstruction of drainage lines.

The Defendant says the encroachment constituted an unreasonable interference with the Defendant’s use and enjoyment of his land.

5. Notice and Prior Conduct

The Defendant says that prior to March 2026 he made multiple verbal requests to the Plaintiff to address the root intrusion.

The Defendant says the Plaintiff failed or refused to take reasonable steps to prevent ongoing damage.

The Defendant denies paragraph 7 of the Statement of Claim.

6. Alleged Poisoning

The Defendant denies intentionally introducing any toxic substance for the purpose of killing the Tree.

The Defendant admits applying a commercially available root-inhibiting compound to soil within his own property boundary.

The Defendant says the compound was applied for the lawful purpose of protecting his property from further damage.

The Defendant denies that the compound was applied negligently or in a manner capable of causing systemic harm beyond the Defendant’s land.

7. Causation

The Defendant denies that his actions caused the death of the Tree.

The Defendant says any decline of the Tree was caused by age, pre-existing condition, or environmental factors beyond the Defendant’s control.

Alternatively, if the Defendant’s actions contributed to the Tree’s decline (which is denied), such contribution was minimal and not the proximate cause of death.

8. Loss and Damage

The Defendant denies paragraphs 17, 18, and 19 of the Statement of Claim.

The Defendant says any loss suffered by the Plaintiff is exaggerated and not reasonably foreseeable.

The Defendant denies liability for emotional distress.

9. Legal Answer

The Defendant says he exercised a lawful right to protect his property from encroaching roots.

The Defendant denies trespass, negligence, or nuisance.

The Defendant says any interference was reasonable, proportionate, and confined to his own land.

10. Relief

The Defendant seeks:
a. Dismissal of the Plaintiff’s claim;
b. Costs of these proceedings;
c. Such further relief as the Court considers appropriate.
